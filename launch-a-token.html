Launch a token

This is the full walkthrough for launching a token, from creating it to handing off liquidity. Most of the work is just a few confirmations — the heavy lifting happens automatically.

Before you start

You'll need a wallet, a little of the network's currency for transaction fees, and a token to launch. This guide covers a FUN launch — the launch style available today — where you create the token as the first step. PUNK launches (bring your own token) are on the roadmap but not live yet.

Step 1 — Create your token

For a FUN launch, you use punk.fun's token creator. You provide a name, a symbol, and a total supply. The entire supply is minted to your wallet. The token is fixed-supply with no owner and no admin powers — that's intentional and cannot be changed afterwards.

Coming soon  PUNK launches will let you skip creation and use an existing token's address instead. That path isn't available yet.

Choosing a supply

Total supply is mostly cosmetic — a token with 1 billion units isn't "worth less" than one with 1 million. What matters is how much value the market assigns to the whole supply. Pick a round number you're happy to see quoted everywhere.

Step 2 — Create the auction

Next you create the auction itself. This is where you set the rules of the sale. You'll pick the token and a handful of auction settings:

SettingWhat it controls
StartWhen the auction becomes active. Can be immediate or a future point.
Starting priceThe price per token at the top of the first tranche.
First tranche sizeHow many tokens are in the first batch.
Price decayHow fast the price falls while a tranche is waiting for buyers.
Minimum priceA floor — the price never falls below this.
Tranche multipliersHow each new tranche changes in size, price, and decay speed compared to the last.

Every setting has a sensible allowed range, so it's hard to create something nonsensical. The launch interface will guide you and show the limits for each field.

What you don't control

Fees, the liquidity destination, and the 50/50 split are all set by the protocol, not by you. This is deliberate — it means buyers can trust those parts are the same for every launch.

Step 3 — Fund the auction

Creating the auction doesn't put any tokens into it. After it exists, you transfer your launch supply into the auction. The launch interface will show you the exact balance the auction has received so you can confirm it's correct before moving on.

Send the right amount

The 50/50 split is calculated from whatever balance is actually in the auction when you confirm it — not from what you intended to send. Double-check the displayed balance before the next step.

Step 4 — Configure the inventory

This is a one-click confirmation. It locks in the split: half of the funded supply becomes the sale allocation, half becomes the liquidity reserve. After this point the split is fixed and cannot be redone.

If the auction's start point has already passed, it goes live immediately. If you set a future start, it waits until then.

Made a mistake?

If you funded the wrong amount, you can withdraw the tokens back out — but only before you configure the inventory. Once configured, withdrawal is closed. Use this escape hatch if the displayed balance looks wrong.

Step 5 — The auction runs

From here, you don't need to do anything. Buyers commit funds, tranches roll over, and the price moves according to the rules you set. You can watch progress, but the sale runs on its own.

Step 6 — Settlement happens automatically

When the sale allocation is fully sold, settlement fires in the same transaction as the final purchase. The proceeds and the liquidity reserve are placed into a trading pool, and buyers can begin claiming their tokens.

In the rare case the automatic step fails for an external reason, anyone can trigger a retry — the sale itself remains valid. You don't lose the raise.

After the launch

Once settled, the token has a live market and the launch is essentially done. What happens to the liquidity position and any ongoing rewards is covered in After the sale.

Reality check

A successful launch needs demand. punk.fun gives you fair mechanics and an automatic market, but it can't create buyers. In this version an auction only completes by selling out — if there isn't enough interest, the sale simply won't finish. Plan your community and marketing before you launch.
