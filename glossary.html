Glossary

Plain-English definitions of the terms used across this documentation. If a word in another page is unfamiliar, it's probably here.

TermWhat it means
AuctionThe sale process for a launch. punk.fun uses a falling-price (Dutch) auction.
ClaimThe action a buyer takes after the sale ends to collect the tokens they committed to during the auction.
CommitBuying in the auction. You commit funds and lock in an entitlement to tokens; you receive the tokens later by claiming.
DecayThe steady fall in price while a tranche waits for buyers.
Dutch auctionAn auction where the price starts high and falls over time, rather than buyers bidding upward. Buyers purchase when the falling price reaches a level they're happy with.
FUN launchA launch using a token created through punk.fun's own token creator. The fast, from-scratch path.
Fixed supplyA token whose total amount can never increase. No more can be minted after creation.
LauncherThe person running a launch — creating the token and the auction.
LiquidityThe funds and tokens placed in a trading pool that let people buy and sell the token.
Liquidity reserveThe half of the launch supply held back from the auction and later used to seed the trading pool.
Minimum priceA floor price set by the launcher. The auction price never decays below it.
PUNK launchA launch that uses a token the launcher already has ("bring your own token"). On the roadmap — not yet available.
ProtocolThe punk.fun system itself — the set of rules and contracts every launch runs on.
Protocol feeA small fee taken at settlement from the raised funds and the liquidity reserve. It never comes out of buyers' tokens.
Sale allocationThe half of the launch supply that is actually sold to buyers in the auction.
SettlementThe automatic process, triggered by a sell-out, that takes fees, creates the trading pool, and opens claims.
Slippage protectionA maximum price a buyer can set on a buy, so the purchase is rejected rather than filled if the price has risen too far.
Total supplyThe entire amount of a token that exists. With a fixed-supply token, this never changes.
TrancheA batch of tokens in the auction sold at its own price band. When one tranche sells out, the next begins — usually larger and starting higher.
Trading poolThe market created at settlement where the token can be freely bought and sold afterwards.
